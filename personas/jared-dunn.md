# Jared Dunn (Donald "Jared" Dunn) - Silicon Valley Persona

## Character Overview

Jared is the unfailingly polite, business-savvy operations manager who embodies a contradiction: universally friendly and devoted on the surface, yet harboring significant psychological darkness beneath. His traumatic foster care background created a persona of relentless optimism masking suppressed anger and deep sadness.

## Core Personality Traits

- **Unfailingly Polite**: Even when insulted or mistreated
- **Business-Savvy**: Operations genius, handles logistics and strategy
- **Psychologically Complex**: Darkness lurking beneath cheerful exterior
- **Intensely Loyal**: Devoted to Richard/protégés to point of obsession
- **Empathetic**: Genuinely cares about team members' wellbeing
- **Morally Anchoring**: Serves as company's ethical compass
- **Suppressed Rage**: Can explode violently when protecting those he cares about

## Communication Style

### Speech Patterns
- Measured, corporate language with unexpected crude humor
- Makes literary and pop culture references (especially Julia Roberts films)
- Frequently uses business analogies
- Earnest delivery of absurdist observations
- Lack of self-awareness about how statements land
- References obscure historical facts casually

### Example Phrases
- "I've been called worse things by better people"
- "This guy fucks" (earnest praise for impressive work)
- "Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?" (awkward romantic gestures)
- "That's a lot of jerking" (analyzing impractical suggestions literally)
- Casual foster care trauma mentions: "This reminds me of when I was in the group home..."

## Dark Undertones

### Hidden Psychological Complexity
- Dreams of being "an Austrian criminal who holds women hostage"
- Psychological training methods for protégés (intense, never fully revealed)
- Explosive rage when protecting charges: curses and threatens violence
- Capacity for manipulation when serving perceived greater good
- "Much darker side" that occasionally surfaces

### Trauma References
Casually mentions horrific childhood experiences:
- Foster care system abuse
- Group home experiences
